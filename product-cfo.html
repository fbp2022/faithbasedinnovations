A financial operating system—not a dashboard toy

CFO is built for money in motion: structure, planning, and decisions grounded in your data. It is not a generic tracker or a thin budgeting app—it helps you interpret, plan, and act from real inputs. Everything rolls up into one coherent picture instead of disconnected categories.

Steward inside CFO reads the records you add, highlights patterns and risks, and helps with projections and runway. Answers follow the same rules every time for the same inputs—nothing is invented to fill gaps.

What CFO handles

  • Budgeting and financial planning
  • Forecasting and cash-flow clarity
  • Calendar-based financial awareness for upcoming and recurring obligations
  • Predictive scheduling of expenses from patterns or user-defined entries
  • Manual financial calendar control when you want full input authority
  • Structured financial database foundation—accounts, transactions, documents, and relationships kept in one coherent model
  • OCR ingestion for statements and documents
  • Optional Plaid integration—explicit and optional, never a silent default
  • Structured financial intelligence and advanced organization

CFO includes a financial calendar that tracks recurring and upcoming expenses, can reflect patterns from your activity, and supports fully manual entry when you prefer direct control. It is there to give forward visibility into obligations—not as a separate gimmick, but as part of how the system keeps time and money aligned.

Local-first and privacy-first. Your financial data stays oriented to your machine and your choices.

Structured foundation

Your money, organized as a real system

CFO is not a stack of flat budgeting screens. Under the experience you use every day is a structured, custom financial database—built in, available to every user, and part of the standard product. It holds accounts, transactions, recurring patterns, uploaded documents, and the relationships between them in one coherent model—not as disconnected tiles.

That foundation is what lets CFO behave as a financial operating system: richer organization, clearer visibility, and room for deeper analysis over time. You do not need to be a power user or a business customer to benefit; it is the same layer whether you want a straightforward tool or a more structured view underneath.

Steward without guesswork

Steward is a controlled, rules-based layer—not vague automation. In CFO, projections and signals trace back to sources and policy.

No fabricated balances or filler numbers. Where information is missing, CFO does not pretend otherwise.

Under the interface, CFO relies on a structured internal financial model that ties accounts, transactions, recurring events, and documents together. It is part of the standard experience for every user, supports consistent tracking and planning, and is not presented as a developer-facing tool.
