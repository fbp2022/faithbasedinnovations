Ledger

Secure vault. Deterministic documents.

Ledger is a local-first encrypted identity vault and deterministic document prefilling system—structured for up to two people (for example, husband and wife). Steward helps parse and map fields—it never guesses or generates answers you did not provide.

Vault first—then documents, filled exactly

Ledger is a local-first encrypted identity vault and deterministic document prefilling system. It is not a “documents tab” inside another app; it is built for sensitive identity data and precise execution.

Stored material can include identity fields (including sensitive items such as SSN where you choose to store them), address history, employment history, relationships, documents, and profiles for up to two people—for example, you and a spouse—structured and encrypted on your machine.

People and references

Ledger includes a People system for those you have known: relationship type, company, role, relevance, and when you met them.

Duration is computed from local system time—so “how long you have known someone” updates naturally; it is never a static string you have to fix by hand.

Deterministic filling—never invented answers

Ledger maps document fields to stored data and fills only exact known values. If a value is missing, Ledger does not guess or infer.

Steward assists with parsing documents, mapping fields, and guiding workflows. It does not generate answers for you. The bar is correctness and auditability, not autocomplete theater.

Alongside CFO and Eden

CFO handles financial operating logic. Eden handles research ingestion and evaluation. Ledger handles identity, references, and document execution—all three premium, local-first, and connected through Steward without collapsing into one generic tool.
